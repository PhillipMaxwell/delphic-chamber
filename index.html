Phillip Maxwell: The Artist

The Art

Where I'm From

I was born in 1973 in Lynnwood Washington. As for where I'm from, well I moved around a bit (call me the Highlander...), from Seattle to Coeur d'Alene Idaho to Bothel Washington and then back to Seattle where I graduated highschool. But art was never a focus in any of my formal education nor did I display artistic talent early on (I got a C in my highschool art classes). My artistic journey began when a co-worker told me that the art doesn't have to be any good, you just have to enjoy creating it and it was from that vantage point that I trained myself.

My Interests

I a have a wide range of interests. I sing in a choir most Sundays, I enjoy photography, watercolor, studying astrology, collecting rocks, making jewelry, playing go, yoga, tai chi, nature walks and watching T.V. shows. Given the scattered array of interests, I don't always have time for all of them. For example it has been some time since I have gone out picture taking.

Artistic philosophy

My philosophy when creating has been fairly simple: at some level I have to feel that what I have created means something even if I don't know what that is. Beyond that I would like to represent something spiritual but when it comes to that aspect of the painting I don't seem to have control as to what that content will be. Thus I often finish a piece and notice a spiritual presence in it that I didn't know I was going to represent. This is occasionally unsettling but there always seems to be a positive spiritual message in the end.

The Artistic Journey

My journey as an artist began in part due to my grandmother suffering a stroke. I was talking to female coworker about my grandmother having suffered a stroke paralyzing my grandma's entire left side of her body. The girl suggested then to use something like watercolor to help stimulate the right brain for healing. I mentioned that I myself wouldn't likely do much painting because I was no good at it but the girl said that she painted from time to time and that it frequently looked like mud, but that what was important was that she enjoyed painting anyway. This planted a seed as I went about getting some basic Crayola Watercolors and watercolor paper to take with me on visits to my grandma to see if I could help her recover brain function through art. Grandma didn't do much painting but I had blast! I soon there after found myself creating all kinds of pieces (very raw initially, likely no composition) in my spare time.

Artistic Influences

Given that I like spiritual elements to come to the surface in my work, I would suspect the spiritual books that I have read are influencing it (not however through consious effort). So books like Eckhart Tolle's "The Power of Now" and "A New Earth" are likely working there way through my subconsious. Also books such as James Redfields "Celestine Prophesy" and his other books that follow factor in as well. These books focus more on the spiritually transcendtal rather than well defined dogmas although I suppose they could be considered dogmas in their own right. As for influence from other artists, I really like Thomas Kinkades work as he does a superb job depicting light in his work. Given that I currently am caught up in trying to give my work a fractal look I am also a big fan of Karin Kuhlmann and her digital art.

Nonstandard Bio

The standard for writing an artistic bio is in the third person. I began to try and write my bio this way but felt artificial, distant, overly formal and impersonal. I don't take my art impersonnally, I hope not to be distant toward those who might like what I do, I am not formally trained, and I have a little bit of a rebel mentality that chafes at rules that I don't see the purpose in. This isn't simply rebelion for it's own sake either, I do have a purpose to write in the first person; I invite you to imagine that you are sitting across from me and I am telling you about myself or my art and in so doing you can make a better personal connection to me and thus my art.
