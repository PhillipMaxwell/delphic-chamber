Mystic Mountain

About

General

This is a 9"x12" watercolor on Arches 140lb cold press 100% cotton rag paper circa 2008.

The Process

I came up with the idea for this probably 2 years before finishing it while experimenting with oil paint. So here’s the scene: I got some canvas in front of me and I’m experimenting with acrylics(it could have been oil paints, my memory is hazy on this point but this is not important in the slightest). I was in a pretty good mood and singing, “Get down! Get down! Get down and boogie!” and other such disco lyrics while chair dancing. I went to clean off the brush on a white tee shirt I designated cleaning rag and in a fit of creativity painted an image very similar to this painting. But I wasn’t ready to make it into a watercolor, and didn’t even realize anything was there, it had to wait probably a good six months before I did anything with it. It was at that point that I was looking at this rag with it’s pretty splotches of color this way and that drew in my eye, all the colors were a bit mesmerizing and realized that there was a darn good idea ready to be mined so I set about drawing a frame work. The frame work went quickly then came masking it which was challenging because I had to use masking fluid on most of it ( it was all I knew at the time) But after all that preparation it sat for a good many months before completing it. And the piece of what looks like gnarled wood on the right? Not even part of the initial frame work, thrown in at the last minute because I thought there needed to be something there due to the blank space. It turns out that it wasn’t entirely necessary but I’m glad it’s in the piece because it is symbolically significant.

The Meaning Part 1

Well lets start with the stump: the only thing here that doesnt look clean, clear and pristine. I show a lot of my pieces to my mom and she had this to say about it: trial. So the piece has all kinds of inspiring spiritual dimensions to access but before I can access it trial must be faced and this means dealing with your own dark side weather that be anger, resentment, fear, hate, jealousy, entitlement you name it, to some extent or the other you do not pass go, do not collect $200 before taking out the trash. And the rest… Well in it you see various sections of “the mountain” and yet it is not entirely a mountain at all! The sections are like realms each one being like another plane of existence.

The Meaning Part 2

To start, there is the region floating just over the rolling hills that is a dark indigo. This is a place of great mystery and wonderment but can be quite perilous if you have not yet dealt properly with the stump. Things on this plain are seen as powerful menacing and brutal when the stump is not properly dealt with but the beings here are really angels in disguise and simply want to assist in lightening the psychological load. There is much of faith in this realm and so long as you have it you will always find relief but indulge in your fears and they will become manifest for you. The key to this realm is meditation as nothing you do to lighten the load can be forced. If you force the issue then the situation gets worse, but relax and let the servants of this realm get to work and they will take from you what you don’t need as you let it go.

The Meaning Part 3

There are two sections directly above this separated by what I might call a white flame, the section on the left and the section on the right. The right section is a realm of simple movement and the folks there teach you how to move so as to be fully focused on that movement and nothing else. Plow the field harvest grain, go about your daily business all the while forgetting about yesterday and not concerning yourself tomorrow, every movement is an end to itself. The beings here are masters of the principle chop wood, carry water and are here to model that for you. The section to the left counterbalances this with insight. Relaxed and receptive this side represents many different planes of ideas that may come to a person . There is a plane of technological innovation, a plane of spiritual insight in action, a plane of spiritual insight in reflection, a plane of complex solutions, and a plane of simple solutions . The white flame between is the point of stillness out of which all the planes arise.

The Meaning Part 4

The figure above that is a man his large head showing that he has a high degree of mastery. The thin path that attaches to his torso is a thread, and it connects to another realm which is our realm, the realm of the day to day world. He is a guide and has synthesized the wisdom of all the realms in his head and leads you to a place of vast knowledge and understanding of all the realms.
